Theoretical Methodology

In Schwarzschild spacetime, the metric near the event horizon generates tidal gravitational gradients that scale as ∝ M/r³. These increasing tidal forces near the horizon can amplify environmental interactions causing quantum decoherence — the irreversible loss of phase coherence in a quantum superposition. This simulator visualizes simplified phenomenological scaling laws connecting distance, black hole mass, and decoherence rate across three distinct physical channels.

Scaling Law Justifications:

  • Thermal Channel (r−1.5): Explicitly includes the Hawking temperature TH = ℏc³/(8πGMkB). Smaller black holes have higher temperatures, causing faster thermal decoherence. Combined with distance scaling from Unruh-like radiation effects.
  • Gravitational Tidal Channel (r−2): Based on tidal force scaling ∝ GM/r³. The enhancement factor (1 + r−3) accounts for strong-field corrections very near the horizon. This term approximates tidal gravitational effects — not gravity itself.
  • Vacuum Channel (r−2.5): QFT in curved spacetime estimates with logarithmic corrections representing vacuum state modifications near strong curvature regions.
  • Mode Comparisons: Classical (information destruction, violates unitarity), Scrambling (fast scrambling conjecture, unitarity-preserving), Entanglement (holographic principle, ER=EPR).

Known Limitations & Uncertainties

  • No Experimental Validation: Direct observation of quantum decoherence near stellar black holes is currently impossible.
  • Simplified Model: Full quantum gravity treatment would require theories beyond current physics (string theory, loop quantum gravity).
  • Parameter Uncertainty: Phenomenological constants have uncertainty ranges of ±50% (thermal) to ±100% (gravitational/vacuum).
  • Classical Approximations: Uses semiclassical gravity; full quantum effects (backreaction, Planck-scale corrections) may differ significantly.
  • No Backreaction: Ignores how quantum decoherence might affect the spacetime geometry itself.
  • Event Horizon Constraint: Simulation is physically meaningful only for r > rs. Inside the horizon, the coordinate system breaks down and classical simulation is invalid.
