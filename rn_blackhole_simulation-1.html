Theoretical Methodology — Reissner-Nordström Framework

The Reissner-Nordström metric is an exact solution of the Einstein-Maxwell equations describing the spacetime exterior to a charged, non-rotating mass. Unlike Schwarzschild, it admits two horizons: an outer event horizon r+ and an inner Cauchy horizon r−. The metric function f(r) = 1 − rs/r + rQ²/r² encodes both gravitational and electromagnetic contributions to spacetime curvature, fundamentally altering the Hawking temperature, time dilation, and decoherence rates compared to Schwarzschild.

Channel Formulas — Fully RN-Corrected (distance normalised to r+):

  • Thermal Channel — ΓT ∝ TH(q) · (r/r+)−1.5 / √f(r): TH(q) = ℏc√(1−q²) / [πkBrs(1+√(1−q²))²] suppresses the rate for increasing charge. The Tolman redshift factor 1/√f(r) represents the local temperature seen by a static observer — it diverges as r → r+. Both factors now carry genuine q-dependence. At extremality TH → 0 so this channel vanishes entirely.
  • Gravitational Tidal Channel — ΓG ∝ |ρ−3 − (3q²/4)ρ−4|: Derived from the RN Weyl tensor electric component Rrtrt ∝ M/r³ − 2kQ²/(c²r⁴). In rs-normalised units this becomes ρ−3 − (3q²/4)ρ−4. The EM stress-energy partially cancels the gravitational tidal term. At extremality with r = r+ = rs/2 both terms exactly equal — net tidal force = 0, consistent with κ = 0. The Schwarzschild formula (ρ−2) ignored the second term entirely.
  • Vacuum Fluctuation Channel — ΓV ∝ (r/r+)−2.5 · log(r/r+) · [1 + 0.6q² log(r+/r−)] / √f(r): Two new RN corrections over the Schwarzschild version: (1) the Tolman factor 1/√f(r) accounts for blue-shifted local vacuum energy near r+; (2) the two-horizon phase space factor [1 + 0.6q² log(r+/r−)] captures the enlarged QFT vacuum between r− and r+. This factor diverges logarithmically as r− → r+ (extremal limit), so near-extremal black holes have strongly enhanced vacuum decoherence despite zero Hawking temperature.
  • Electromagnetic Channel — ΓEM ∝ q²(r/r+)−4: Unique to RN. Coulomb energy density uEM = ε₀E²/2 ∝ Q²/r⁴. Now normalised to r+ so it correctly diverges at the outer horizon. Absent at q=0. Dominant near extremality where the tidal and thermal channels are suppressed.
  • Distance Normalisation Fix: All channels now use ρphys = r/r+ internally rather than r/rs. Since r+ < rs for any q > 0, the old formula systematically underestimated proximity to the true horizon. The chart x-axis remains in r/rs for readability.
  • Extremal Limit (q=1) — channel competition: TH → 0 kills thermal; tidal Weyl term cancels at r+; but vacuum channel diverges logarithmically (log(r+/r−) → ∞) and EM channel peaks at r+. Net decoherence is dominated by vacuum + EM channels, not thermal — a distinctly non-Schwarzschild result.

Known Limitations & Physical Caveats

  • Astrophysical RN Black Holes: Real black holes are expected to be approximately electrically neutral — accreted plasma quickly neutralizes any net charge. RN is primarily a theoretical/pedagogical model.
  • Strong Cosmic Censorship: The Cauchy horizon r− is unstable to perturbations. The strong SCC conjecture predicts r− is actually a singularity — the interior shown is classically idealized.
  • Schwinger Mechanism: Near r+, strong electric fields can pair-produce charged particles (Schwinger effect), rapidly discharging the black hole. This non-perturbative effect is not modeled.
  • No Rotation: Real astrophysical black holes are described by Kerr-Newman (charged + rotating). RN ignores spin entirely.
  • Semiclassical Approximation: Full quantum gravity (string theory, LQG) would alter the near-extremal and near-horizon physics significantly.
  • EM Channel Phenomenology: The EM decoherence amplitude (~3×10⁻⁵) carries ±100% uncertainty. The q²r⁻⁴ scaling is motivated by EM energy density but lacks experimental confirmation near BHs.
