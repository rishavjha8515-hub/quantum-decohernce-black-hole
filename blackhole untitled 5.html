Theoretical Methodology - PHYSICS-CONNECTED MODEL

Scaling Law Justifications:

  • Thermal Channel (r⁻¹·⁵): Explicitly includes Hawking temperature T_H = ℏc³/(8πGMk_B). Smaller black holes have higher temperatures and cause faster thermal decoherence. Combined with distance scaling from Unruh-like effects.
  • Gravitational Channel (r⁻²): Based on tidal force scaling. Enhancement factor (1 + r⁻³) accounts for strong-field corrections very near the horizon.
  • Vacuum Channel (r⁻²·⁵): QFT in curved spacetime estimates with logarithmic corrections representing vacuum state modifications.
  • Visual Decay Rate: Particle coherence directly driven by model.decoherenceRate() with pedagogical time scaling for visibility.

Reference Framework: Hawking (1975), Zurek (2003), dimensional analysis. Phenomenological constants calibrated for observable timescales.

⚠️ Visual Scaling Note

Particle decay rates are accelerated ~10× for visualization purposes. Actual decoherence timescales near black holes range from microseconds (near horizon) to seconds (far field). We compress these for educational clarity while preserving relative scaling relationships.

Known Limitations & Uncertainties

  • No Experimental Validation: Direct observation of quantum decoherence near black holes is currently impossible.
  • Simplified Model: Full quantum gravity treatment would require theories beyond current physics (string theory, loop quantum gravity).
  • Parameter Uncertainty: Phenomenological constants have uncertainty ranges of ±50% (thermal) to ±100% (gravitational/vacuum).
  • Classical Approximations: Uses semiclassical gravity; full quantum effects may differ significantly.
  • No Backreaction: Ignores how quantum decoherence might affect spacetime geometry itself.
